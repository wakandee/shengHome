Categories

Select a category to explore Sheng words and their translations:

Nouns

Nouns represent people, places, or things. Sheng nouns evolve from English, Kiswahili, and local languages.

  • Msee – Person
  • Keja – House

Verbs

Verbs describe actions, often mixing English and Kiswahili with Sheng flair.

  • Kusota – To suffer or struggle
  • Chillax – To relax

Adverbs

Adverbs modify verbs and provide more context to actions in Sheng sentences.

  • Haraka – Quickly
  • Mbaya – Badly

Adjectives

Adjectives describe qualities of nouns, often adding a playful Sheng twist to regular words.

  • Mafresh – Cool or stylish
  • Mbichi – Raw or unpolished

Special Categories

Shembeteng

Shembeteng is a playful twist of Sheng, altering words by inserting extra syllables.

  • Seve – Love
  • Keteng – Ken

Conflicting Words

These are Sheng words that carry different meanings depending on context.

  • Nganya – Can mean either a "matatu" or a "crazy person"
  • Chapaa – Can mean "money" or "to print something"
